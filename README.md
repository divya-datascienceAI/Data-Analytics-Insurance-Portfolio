# 🚗 Auto Insurance Claims Analytics Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an interactive Power BI dashboard that analyzes auto insurance policies and claims data. The dashboard provides insights into customer profiles, policy coverage, claim trends, fraud patterns, and financial performance to support data-driven decision-making for insurance companies.

---

## 🎯 Business Objective

The objective of this project is to:

- Analyze insurance claim trends and customer behavior.
- Identify factors contributing to high claim amounts.
- Monitor fraud rates across different customer segments.
- Evaluate policy coverage and premium performance.
- Support underwriting and risk management decisions.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- **Power BI** – Data Visualization
- **Power Query** – Data Cleaning & Transformation
- **DAX** – Measures & Calculations
- **Excel** – Data Source

## 📈 Key Insights

- Customers aged 30–45 years account for the highest number of insurance claims.
- Single-vehicle and three-vehicle accidents generate higher claim amounts than two- or four-vehicle accidents.
- Policyholders with Ph.D. and Master's degrees have the highest fraud rates among all education levels.
- Executive Managers exhibit the highest fraud rate compared to other occupations.
- New York records the highest total claim amount, while Ohio has the lowest.
- Single-vehicle accidents are the most frequently reported incidents.
- The insurance portfolio generated an overall profit of approximately 1.26 million.
- Vehicle collision incidents result in significantly higher claim amounts than vehicle theft incidents.
- Property claims represent the largest portion of total claims across all vehicle models, with Dodge recording the highest claim amount.

## 💡 Business Recommendations

1. **Develop targeted risk assessment strategies for customers aged 30–45**, as this segment contributes the highest number of claims. Insurance providers can review pricing models and offer personalized risk management programs for this age group.

2. **Analyze accident severity and vehicle involvement patterns** to improve underwriting decisions. Higher claim amounts from accidents involving one or three vehicles indicate the need for better risk evaluation and coverage pricing.

3. **Strengthen fraud monitoring processes for high-risk customer segments**, especially policyholders with Ph.D. and Master's education levels, by implementing additional claim verification checks.

4. **Conduct deeper fraud investigations among Executive Managers**, as this occupation shows a higher fraud rate. Additional validation steps can help reduce fraudulent claim losses.

5. **Optimize regional risk strategies by analyzing state-level claim patterns**, especially in high-claim states like New York. Insurance companies can adjust pricing, coverage recommendations, and risk mitigation strategies based on geographic trends.

6. **Improve single-vehicle accident risk assessment**, as these incidents represent the highest number of claims. Insurers can focus on driver behavior analysis and preventive safety programs.

7. **Maintain profitability through continuous monitoring of premium collection and claim payouts**, while identifying opportunities to reduce unnecessary claim expenses.

8. **Focus underwriting strategies on collision-related risks**, as vehicle collisions generate higher claim costs than theft incidents. Insurers can provide safety recommendations and adjust premiums based on collision risk.

9. **Review vehicle-specific risk factors, especially for high-claim models like Dodge**, by analyzing repair costs, accident frequency, and claim severity to support accurate vehicle pricing and coverage decisions.
